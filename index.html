Dissertation project: "The Houses of Soviets: Soviet Architecture and Socio-political Change of 1920-30s"

The House of Soviets in Bryansk

It was built between 1921 and 1927.

It was designed by the well-known Soviet avant-garde architect Alexander Grinberg in the modern style with the simplified traditionalist elements.

It was the first Soviet government building ever built.

The Government House of Soviets in Makhachkala

The building was erected between 1921 and 1930.

It was designed by one of the main advocates of the neoclassicism and traditionalism in the Soviet architecture, Ivan Zholtovsky. As one of the tasks was to express the national character of Dagestan. Zholtovsky integrated the elements of the Islamic architecture such as the pointed arches.

The building has never served its original purpose.

The House of Government of Kazakh ASSR in Almaty

The building was constructed between 1927 and 1931.

  • Architect: Moisej Ginzburg
  • Style: Constructivism
The House of Government of BSSR in Minsk

The construction was erected between 1927 and 1933 in Minsk

  • Architect: Iosif Langbard
  • Style: Modernism
The House of Soviets in Elista

The building was erected between 1928 and 1933 in Elista.

  • Architect: Iliya Golosov
  • Style: Constructivism
